<div id="section-6">
  <SectionTitle title="6) Common Mistakes That Tank Your Acceptance Rate">
    After 8 years of doing this, I've made every mistake in the book. Probably twice.
  </SectionTitle>

  <p>**Mistake 1: Using "I'd like to add you to my network"**</p>
  <p>This is LinkedIn's default. It screams "I didn't care enough to write something." Generic requests see roughly **15% acceptance**. Ouch.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 2: Pitching in the connection request**</p>
  <p>Nothing tanks acceptance faster than "I'd love to show you how we can help..." Just don't. Save the pitch. Get accepted first.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 3: No clear reason to connect**</p>
  <p>Why should they care? "I noticed we're both in sales" isn't a reason. "I saw your approach to cold outreach in [specific post]" is. See the difference?</p>

  <p>**Mistake 4: Incomplete profile**</p>
  <p>No photo? Missing headline? Bare summary? You're starting with a credibility deficit. Remember that 3-7 second decision? They scan your profile, not just your message.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 5: Connecting with 3rd-degree strangers cold**</p>
  <p>Without mutual connections or shared context, you're basically a cold caller. Acceptance rates plummet.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 6: Sending too many too fast**</p>
  <p>LinkedIn allows roughly **100-200 requests per week** depending on your account. Push past that and you'll trigger restrictions. Start with **20-30 per day** to stay safe. Trust me on this one.</p>
