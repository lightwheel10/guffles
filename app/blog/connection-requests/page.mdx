  <p>*Why it works*: you're referencing something they actually did, and that flash of recognition is what tips them toward accepting.</p>

  <div className="not-prose my-4 p-4 bg-muted rounded-lg border-l-4 border-primary">

  </div>
  <p>*Why it works*: a shared connection borrows their trust, so you're not starting from zero.</p>

  <h3>Template 3: Same Event/Webinar</h3>
  <div className="not-prose my-4 p-4 bg-muted rounded-lg border-l-4 border-primary">

  </div>
  <p>*Why it works*: a shared experience gives you instant common ground, and it's hard to feel like a stranger to someone who was in the same room.</p>

  <h3>Template 4: Content Value Add</h3>
  <div className="not-prose my-4 p-4 bg-muted rounded-lg border-l-4 border-primary">

  </div>
  <p>*Why it works*: you're leading with something useful instead of a pitch, and people accept generosity far more readily than asks.</p>

<div id="section-6">
  <SectionTitle title="6) Common Mistakes That Tank Your Acceptance Rate">
    After 8 years of this, I've personally made every one of these, several of them more than once.
  </SectionTitle>

  <p>**Mistake 1: Using "I'd like to add you to my network"**</p>
  <p>This is LinkedIn's default text, and it quietly tells the reader you didn't care enough to write anything. Generic requests like this land around 15% acceptance, which is a steep price for saving thirty seconds.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 2: Pitching in the connection request**</p>
  <p>Nothing tanks acceptance faster than "I'd love to show you how we can help..." The pitch can wait. Get the connection first, earn a little trust, and then make your case once you're actually in their network.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 3: No clear reason to connect**</p>
  <p>Give them a reason to care. "I noticed we're both in sales" isn't one, but "I saw your approach to cold outreach in [specific post]" is, because it shows you did the homework rather than mass-adding everyone with a matching title.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 4: Incomplete profile**</p>
  <p>No photo, a missing headline, or a bare summary all leave you starting from a credibility deficit. Remember that 3-7 second decision: people scan your profile as much as your message, so a thin profile sinks even a great note.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 5: Connecting with 3rd-degree strangers cold**</p>
  <p>Without a mutual connection or any shared context, you're effectively a cold caller, and acceptance rates fall off a cliff. This is exactly the gap that engaging with someone's content first is meant to close.</p>

  <p>**Mistake 6: Sending too many too fast**</p>
  <p>LinkedIn allows roughly 100-200 requests per week depending on your account, and pushing past that will trigger restrictions you do not want. Start with 20-30 per day to stay on the safe side; I've had an account throttled for getting greedy, and it's not worth the week of limited reach.</p>
