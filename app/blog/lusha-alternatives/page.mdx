  <p>An intent-based lead is a prospect you identify through their online engagement and behavior signals, which point to active interest in a topic or product, rather than a name pulled from a static list.</p>

  <p>The logic isn't complicated. Someone who commented on a competitor's post yesterday is warmer than a contact scraped from a database six months ago. According to <a href="https://www.factors.ai/blog/intent-data-platforms-vs-traditional-lead-generation" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Factors.ai</a>, intent-driven leads convert at 2-3x the rate of traditional leads (20-25% vs 5-10%). That is a different value proposition, not a cheaper version of the same one. If you want to dig into this category, our <Link href="/blog/intent-data-providers">intent data providers comparison</Link> goes deeper.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">1. Apollo.io - Best All-in-One Platform</h3>

  <p>Apollo is the Swiss Army knife of sales tools. With 275M+ contacts and built-in email sequences, it's far more than a contact database, and pricing starts at $49/month for 5,000 credits, with an unlimited tier at $79/month. The flip side of all that capability is that it's overkill if you only need quick LinkedIn lookups. I've watched teams drown in the feature set when what they actually wanted was an email address. If you're leaning this way, our <Link href="/blog/apollo-alternatives">Apollo alternatives guide</Link> goes into more detail.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">2. UpLead - Best for Data Accuracy</h3>

  <p>UpLead's whole pitch is the 95% accuracy guarantee, and they back it by refunding credits for bounced emails. At $99/month for 170 credits, you're paying more per contact, but the premium buys you quality. It's the right call for teams that are sick of bad data eating their week, and bad data eats a lot of week.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">3. RocketReach - Best for Database Size</h3>

  <p>If raw volume is what you're after, RocketReach has 700M+ profiles. At $69/month you get 1,200 exports per year, and that annual cap is the thing to watch, because it forces you to plan your usage rather than prospect on impulse. It works best for one-off research projects or the kind of prospecting you do in bursts.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">4. Hunter.io - Best for Email Finding</h3>

  <p>Hunter does one thing, email finding, and does it well. The domain search feature is genuinely useful for account-based campaigns, and at $49/month for 500 searches it's easy on the budget. Just don't come to it for phone numbers, because that was never what it was built for.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">5. Kaspr - Best for European Data</h3>

  <p>Kaspr is strong for EU-based prospecting and takes GDPR compliance seriously. At $49-79/month with 1,200+ phone credits, it's solid value if your market is Europe. It's a lot less compelling if your ICP sits mostly in the US.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">6. Cognism - Best for Phone Numbers</h3>

  <p>If you live on the phone, Cognism's 97% phone accuracy is hard to beat, and their "Diamond Data" feature verifies mobile numbers manually instead of trusting the database. The cost of that quality is enterprise pricing starting at $15K/year, which puts it out of reach for small teams and early-stage startups. I found that out myself the first time I asked for a quote.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">7. Seamless.AI - Best for AI-Powered Search</h3>

  <p>Seamless uses AI to find and verify contacts in real time, and at $147+/month it isn't cheap. What you're paying for is fresher data, since the verification happens on the fly rather than against a stale snapshot. It suits teams that have the budget and want the newest features, as long as you go in clear-eyed about the price.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">8. LeadIQ - Best for Salesforce Users</h3>

  <p>LeadIQ's universal credit system and deep Salesforce integration make it a natural fit for enterprise sales teams, with pricing that starts at $36/month. The one caveat is that same credit system, which can get genuinely confusing. I've talked to reps who couldn't tell me what they were actually being charged for.</p>

  <h3 className="text-xl font-semibold mt-8 mb-3">9. Snov.io - Best Budget Option</h3>

It's no-frills, but it does the job, and for an early-stage team counting every dollar that's often exactly the right trade. <div id="hidden-cost">
  <h2 className="text-2xl font-bold mt-12 mb-4">4) The Hidden Cost of Credit-Based Pricing</h2>

  <p>When I was running demand gen at my last startup, we made a mistake I see everywhere: we were measuring the wrong thing.</p>

  <p>We tracked cost per contact, and we high-fived every time we found a cheaper database. What we never tracked was cost per response, or cost per meeting booked. The day we finally ran those numbers, the whole picture flipped.</p>

  <p>The data lines up with that experience. According to <a href="https://belkins.io/blog/cold-email-response-rates" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Belkins' 2024 study</a> of 16.5 million cold emails, average reply rates fell to 5.1%, down from 7% the year before. And <a href="https://www.landbase.com/blog/b2b-contact-data-accuracy-statistic" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Landbase research</a> finds that sales reps lose 500 hours a year, the equivalent of 62 working days, to bad prospect data.</p>

  <p>Add it up and you're paying for contacts that go 22.5% outdated every year, sending into lists where about 5% reply, and losing weeks to data that was never any good. The credit model isn't really the villain here. The cold contact approach is the thing worth questioning.</p>

  <p>If you're burning credits because you're blasting people who never reply, swapping vendors won't save you. The actual issue is that most of those people were never interested to begin with.</p>

  <p>Guffles comes at it from the other direction. At $79/month (with $150 in wallet credit to start), it finds people already engaging with relevant LinkedIn content, the ones who've raised a hand by commenting, liking, or sharing posts in your industry. Across our data from 200+ B2B companies, those warm leads convert at 14.6% versus 1.7% for cold outreach. That gap isn't a tweak at the margins; it's a different game.</p>

  <p>Set that against Apollo ($49-119/month with credit limits), Lusha ($29-70/month with 250-800 credits), or ZoomInfo ($15K+/year). The useful question was never which tool is cheapest per contact. It's which approach actually puts meetings on your calendar.</p>

<div id="how-to-choose">
  <h2 className="text-2xl font-bold mt-12 mb-4">5) How to Choose the Right Alternative</h2>

  <p>No single tool is right for every team. Here's the framework I walk people through, and I've sat in this exact conversation with dozens of teams:</p>

  <p><strong>If you need high volume</strong> (10,000+ contacts monthly): go with Apollo's unlimited tier at $79/month, or Seamless.AI.</p>

  <p><strong>If you need guaranteed accuracy</strong>: UpLead's 95% guarantee or Cognism's 97% phone accuracy earns its premium, because fewer bounces means less time wasted chasing dead addresses.</p>

  <p><strong>If you're targeting Europe</strong>: Kaspr or Cognism give you GDPR-compliant data with strong EU coverage.</p>

  <p><strong>If you live in Salesforce</strong>: nothing matches LeadIQ's integration for enterprise workflows.</p>

  <p><strong>If you want a different approach entirely</strong>: read up on <Link href="/blog/buyer-intent-signals-prospects-ready-to-buy">buyer intent signals that indicate prospects ready to buy</Link>. This is the territory where intent-based tools like Guffles, or even plain manual engagement tracking, can beat the volume-based players.</p>
</div>

<div id="conclusion">
  <h2 className="text-2xl font-bold mt-12 mb-4">So Which One Should You Pick</h2>

  <p>Eight years into B2B sales, the thing I keep coming back to is that the tool matters less than the approach.</p>

  <p>If you just want cheaper contact data than Lusha, Apollo or Snov.io will get you there. If accuracy is the sore spot, UpLead or Cognism are worth what they cost.</p>

  <p>But if your real complaint is burning credits on contacts who never reply, switching vendors probably won't fix it. The problem might be cold outreach itself. I've been on that treadmill, and it wears you down.</p>

  <p><Link href="/blog/warm-vs-cold-outreach-data-breakdown">Warm vs cold outreach data</Link> consistently shows engaged leads converting better. If the intent-based approach feels right for your team, you can even start by hand: watch who engages with competitor posts on LinkedIn and reach out referencing what they did. It works, it's just slow and tedious.</p>

  <p>Or you skip the manual grind. Guffles automates that whole loop at $79/month, and new users get $150 in wallet credit to try it out. Either way, moving from cold contacts to warm engagement is worth testing.</p>

  <p>So the decision isn't really about which Lusha alternative costs the least. It's whether you want more contacts or better ones. For me, that's the question that ended up changing how I sell.</p>
