<div id="reason-1">
    <SectionTitle title="2) Reason 1: You're Targeting People With Zero Buying Intent">
        <p>This is the big one, and it's where most campaigns go off the rails before anyone has written a single line of copy. By the time the email exists, the targeting has already failed.</p>
        <p>Cold email, by definition, means contacting people who have shown no interest in what you sell. They never visited your site, never touched your content, never signaled anything at all. You're interrupting a stranger and betting they happen to need your product the exact week you showed up. Why would they reply to a problem they've never told you they have?</p>
        <p>The data is pretty blunt about it. According to <a href="https://www.landbase.com/blog/lead-qualification-statistics">Landbase's 2025 research</a>, companies that use intent data are 4x more accurate at spotting sales-ready prospects. Getting a feel for the <Link href="/blog/buyer-intent-signals-prospects-ready-to-buy">buyer intent signals that show readiness</Link> is where the fix starts.</p>
        <p>And then there's the part that really stings: modern B2B buyers get through 70% of their buying process before they ever talk to a seller. So by the time your cold email lands, they've usually already decided, or they were never in the market to begin with.</p>
        <p>That's the actual root cause. The bad subject lines, the wrong timing, the generic copy, all of it is just downstream of emailing people who were never going to care.</p>
    </SectionTitle>
</div>

<div id="reason-2">

        <p>The useful question isn't "how do I personalize at scale?" It's "who is even worth personalizing for?"</p>

<div id="reason-3">
    <SectionTitle title="4) Reason 3: Your Emails Never Reach the Inbox">
        <p>Before you go blaming your copy, look at your plumbing. I've watched whole campaigns flatline for the dumbest reason imaginable: nobody ever set up DMARC.</p>
        <p>According to <a href="https://martal.ca/b2b-cold-email-statistics-lb/">Martal Group</a>, 17% of cold emails never reach the inbox at all. They get blocked, bounced, or quietly filed into spam before the prospect has a chance to see them.</p>
        <p>This got noticeably harder in late 2025. Gmail shifted from soft enforcement to full blocking of noncompliant mail. If you skip SPF, DKIM, and DMARC, Gmail no longer just shuffles you to spam, it rejects the message outright. Yahoo rolled out similar rules right behind it.</p>
        <p>Then there's the complaint ceiling. Gmail blocks senders who go past 0.3% spam complaints, which means 3 angry recipients out of every 1,000 is enough to sink you.</p>
        <p>So when people ask why their cold emails land in spam, it's usually some mix of missing authentication, high bounce rates, spam-trigger language, and tripping that complaint threshold. Get these basics right before you touch anything else, because none of your clever optimizations matter if the email never arrives.</p>
    </SectionTitle>

        <p>There's an endless debate about the best day and time to send. Tuesday versus Wednesday, early morning versus mid-afternoon, and so on. Most of it misses the point.</p>
        <p>According to <a href="https://martal.ca/b2b-cold-email-statistics-lb/">Martal Group</a>, multi-channel outreach lifts results by 287% over single-channel. Pairing email with LinkedIn and a phone call simply beats email on its own, and it isn't close.</p>
        <p><a href="https://blog.hubspot.com/sales/hubspot-sales-strategy-report">HubSpot's 2025 State of Sales report</a> says the same thing: 42% of sales pros report social media as their highest-response channel, compared with 26% for email. LinkedIn InMail lands somewhere around 10-25% response rates next to cold email's 1-5%, which is a gap you can drive a truck through.</p>
        <p>The thing that actually moves timing isn't the day of the week. It's whether you reach out while someone is still interested. I think of it as the 72-Hour Engagement Window. Our own data shows that contacting someone within 72 hours of their engagement lifts response rates by 5-7x versus waiting a week, and once you're past 72 hours, replies sink right back to cold levels.
