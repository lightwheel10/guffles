    Quick definitions first, because how you define these terms changes how you measure them.

  <p>Cold outreach means contacting people with no prior relationship or stated interest. You're starting from zero and hoping to spark something. Bought lists, mass connection requests, blasts to strangers, that's all cold.</p>

  <p>Warm outreach means contacting people who've already signalled interest, through engagement, a referral, or reading your content. They did something that suggests they'd actually take your message. The first move is learning to <Link href="/blog/find-warm-leads-social-engagement-signals">find warm leads through social engagement signals</Link>.</p>

  <p>Intent-based prospecting means picking leads off the buying signals they show through behaviour, not just their demographics. Someone commenting on a post about sales tools is showing intent. Someone who fits your ICP perfectly but has engaged with nothing is just a cold lead with a nice job title.</p>

  <h3>The Role of Engagement Signals</h3>

  <p>Engagement signals are really just public intent data. A like, a comment, a share, all of it visible to anyone paying attention.</p>

  <p>When someone comments on a post about CRM software, they're telling you the topic is on their mind. When they like a sales-tools comparison, that's interest too. This is the bridge from a cold list to an actual conversation.</p>

  <p>Tracking this by hand is fine for five or ten posts a week. Past that it falls apart. I tried, and it was hours of scrolling through engagers, opening profiles one at a time, and pasting names into a spreadsheet until my eyes glazed over.</p>

<div id="section-4">
  <SectionTitle title="4) The Engagement-to-Pipeline Formula">
    This is the framework I use to run the numbers for any team. I call it the engagement-to-pipeline formula.
  </SectionTitle>

  <p>For cold outreach:</p>
  <p><strong>Cold ROI = (Meetings Booked x Close Rate x Deal Value) / (Emails Sent x Cost Per Email + SDR Time)</strong></p>

  <p>For warm outreach:</p>
  <p><strong>Warm ROI = (Meetings Booked x Close Rate x Deal Value) / (Signals Captured x Cost Per Signal + SDR Time)</strong></p>

  <p>Here's a concrete example. You send 1,000 cold emails at $0.02 each, plus two hours of SDR time at $50 total. You book 5 meetings (a 0.5% meeting rate) and close one deal at $10,000.</p>

  <p>Cold ROI = ($10,000) / ($20 + $100), which works out to an 83x return.</p>

  <p>Now the warm version. You capture 200 engagement signals at $0.40 each, same two hours of SDR time. You book 20 meetings (a 10% meeting rate) and close 3 deals at $10,000 each.</p>

  <p>Warm ROI = ($30,000) / ($80 + $100), a 167x return.</p>

  <p>Same SDR hours, roughly 2x the ROI, and I lowballed the warm numbers to stay conservative.</p>
</div>

  <p>One detail matters more than it should: timing. It took me embarrassingly long to take it seriously.</p>

  <p>Reaching out within 72 hours of someone engaging lifts response rates by 5-7x compared to waiting a week. The context is still fresh; they remember the post and how they felt about it.</p>

  <p>The sequence is simple:</p>
