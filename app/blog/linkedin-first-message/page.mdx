<div id="7-templates">
  <h2>7 LinkedIn First Message Templates That Actually Get Replies</h2>

  <p>One number to keep in mind before you read these: LinkedIn data shows shorter messages, under 400 characters, get 22% more replies than longer ones. Every template below stays under that limit so it's quick to scan and reads fine on a phone.</p>

  <h3>Template 1: The Grateful Opener</h3>
  <p><em>Best for: When you don't have a specific angle</em></p>
  <blockquote>
      Hey [Name], thanks for connecting! I noticed you're [specific observation]. How are you finding [relevant topic]?
  </blockquote>
  <p>The whole thing hinges on that specific observation. Don't just say "thanks for connecting," add something real, like "I noticed you just moved into a product role at Stripe" or "I saw you've been in fintech sales for a few years." Make it obvious you actually looked at their profile. It takes about 30 seconds and it's worth it.</p>

  <h3>Template 2: The Content Reference</h3>
  <p><em>Best for: When they actively post or comment</em></p>
  <blockquote>
      Hey [Name], your post about [topic] really stuck with me - especially [specific point]. I've been thinking about [related angle]. Have you found [question related to their content]?
  </blockquote>
  <p>This is my highest-converting template by a wide margin, because referencing someone's content proves you actually pay attention instead of just harvesting connections. I've botched this one too, firing off generic messages to people whose posts I should have mentioned, which is embarrassing to think about now.</p>

  <h3>Template 3: The Shared Interest</h3>
  <p><em>Best for: Mutual connections or shared background</em></p>
  <blockquote>
      Hey [Name], noticed we both [shared connection/background]. How's [relevant current situation] going for you?
  </blockquote>
  <p>Same school, same previous employer, same career pivot: any of these creates instant rapport. I once got a 10-message thread going just because we both worked at the same company five years apart. People genuinely enjoy talking about shared experiences.</p>

  <h3>Template 4: The Direct Value</h3>
  <p><em>Best for: When you genuinely have something useful</em></p>
  <blockquote>
      Hey [Name], I came across [resource/insight] and immediately thought of your work in [their area]. Figured it might be useful. No agenda - just thought you'd find it interesting.
  </blockquote>
  <p>The "no agenda" part matters, and you have to actually mean it. The second you follow up with a pitch, you've burned the trust you just built. I've watched reps do exactly that and torch a perfectly good relationship in one move.</p>

  <h3>Template 5: The Networking Play</h3>
  <p><em>Best for: Genuine relationship-building</em></p>
  <blockquote>
      Hey [Name], I've been following your journey in [their space] - really interesting path. Would love to hear how you got into [specific aspect] sometime.
  </blockquote>
  <p>Pure curiosity with no hidden agenda. Sometimes that's exactly what the moment calls for, and not every connection needs to turn into a customer.</p>

  <h3>Template 6: The Recruiter Special</h3>
  <p><em>Best for: Recruiter-to-candidate outreach</em></p>
  <blockquote>
      Hey [Name], your background in [specific skill] caught my attention. I'm working on a [role type] opportunity at [company type] that might align. Would you be open to a quick chat about what you're looking for in your next move?
  </blockquote>
  <p>Worth noting: Belkins' data shows HR and talent acquisition professionals respond at the highest rate of any role, 12.08%. They're used to this kind of outreach, since fielding it is literally their job.</p>

  <h3>Template 7: The Sales Intro</h3>
  <p><em>Best for: Potential customers (but don't pitch yet!)</em></p>
  <blockquote>
      Hey [Name], noticed [specific observation about their company/role]. I work with similar [companies/teams] on [general area], but honestly I'm curious - how are you currently handling [relevant challenge]?
  </blockquote>
  <p>Notice what's missing from this template: a pitch. You're opening a conversation, not closing a deal. I've watched more salespeople than I can count blow their shot by pitching in message one, so build the rapport first and let the sale come later.</p>

  <Callout title="Key Takeaway" icon={<Target className="w-5 h-5 text-primary" />}>
      Keep messages under 400 characters for <strong>22% more replies</strong>. The Content Reference template (mentioning their recent post) converts best because it proves you pay attention.
  </Callout>
</div>

<div id="best-practices">
  <h2>Quick Best Practices (Things That Actually Move the Needle)</h2>

  <p>I've tested a lot of tactics over the years and most of them barely register. These seven actually move the number:</p>

  <p><strong>1. Keep it under 400 characters.</strong> That's about 3-4 sentences, and LinkedIn data shows 22% more replies for shorter messages. People are busy, so respect their time.</p>

  <p><strong>2. Visit their profile before messaging.</strong> Campaigns that combine profile visits with messages get 11.87% reply rates versus 4.88% for messages alone, a 143% improvement. Five seconds of work that roughly doubles your odds.</p>

  <p><strong>3. Send on Tuesday or Monday.</strong> Belkins' data shows Tuesday at 6.90% reply rate leads every other day. Avoid weekends. I used to think Sundays were great for prep, but they're a dead zone.</p>

  <p><strong>4. Never pitch in message one.</strong> I know you're excited about your product, but pitching in the first message kills engagement. Wait 2-3 exchanges and build the relationship first.</p>

  <p><strong>5. End with a question, not a statement.</strong> Questions invite responses. It sounds obvious, yet I still see people closing with "Let me know if you're interested," which isn't a question at all, it's a dead end.</p>

  <p><strong>6. Use contractions and a conversational tone.</strong> "I'm curious about..." beats "I am curious about..." every time. You're having a conversation, not writing a press release, so sound like yourself.</p>

  <p><strong>7. One clear ask, maximum.</strong> Don't ask for a call and their thoughts and a look at your content all in one message. Pick one thing, because more asks mean fewer replies. For more tactical advice, see our <Link href="/blog/15-linkedin-lead-generation-tips">15 LinkedIn lead generation tips</Link>.</p>
