<div id="section-3">
    <SectionTitle title="3) The 4-Step Poll-to-Lead Process">
        I've run more than 50 LinkedIn polls this past year, and the process I keep coming back to for turning votes into conversations is pretty simple.

        <strong>Step 1: Create a Poll That Reveals Pain Points</strong>

        Preference polls like "Coffee or tea?" are fun, but they're useless for sales because they tell you nothing you can act on. Ask questions that expose a challenge instead, things like "What's your biggest obstacle to hitting quota?" or "Where does your lead gen process break down?" Stick to 3-4 options and always include "Other - tell me in comments" to pull a few more people into the conversation.

        <strong>Step 2: Engage While the Poll Runs</strong>

        Don't post and ghost. When people comment, reply, and ask a follow-up question while you're there. It nudges your visibility through the algorithm and it starts warming the relationship before you ever land in someone's inbox. I aim to answer every comment within 2 hours, which some days is genuinely exhausting, but the payoff shows up later.

        <strong>Step 3: Extract Voter Data Within 24 Hours</strong>

        This is where most people stall out. The manual route is to click the vote count, open each profile in a new tab, and note their details, which works fine until you realize you're capped at maybe <strong>5-10 leads per hour</strong>. For a poll with 75 votes, that is a brutal amount of clicking, and I'm speaking from experience.

        <strong>Step 4: Send Personalized Outreach Based on Vote Choice</strong>

        This is where it pays off. Skip the generic "Hey, saw you voted on my poll" and reference the actual choice they made: "Hey Sarah - noticed you voted that budget is your biggest challenge. Curious, is that about headcount or tools?" It's specific, it's relevant, and it makes it obvious you actually paid attention.
